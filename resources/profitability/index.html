The Empathy

Paint the familiar scene: a vendor finishes their first craft fair, stares at a stack of notes, and wonders if the long hours were worth it. The cash box looks full, but once they subtract the booth fee, materials, gas, and snacks, the numbers feel fuzzy. Some shows feel like a win, some feel like a flop, and they don't know what a "good" day actually looks like. The uncertainty makes it hard to choose which fairs to try next.

The Education

Explain the core idea: profitability is more than total sales. Net profit means sales minus every event cost, plus a realistic value for time. Walk through a simple break-even example so vendors can see how booth fee, travel, and inventory costs set a minimum sales target. Mention that comparing net profit per hour makes it easier to judge whether a market is worth repeating or if a different event might be a better fit.

The Solution

Guide them toward a repeatable system: log every event's sales, costs, and hours so each show ends with a clear net number. With that history, vendors can spot patterns, set realistic goals, and decide whether to scale up, stay steady, or pass on a high-fee event. The right system makes it easy to answer the big questions about break-even, profit targets, and whether a fair is helping the business grow.
