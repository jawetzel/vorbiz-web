Profitability question

How do you account for one-time setup costs?

Treat setup expenses like an investment that supports many events, not a single market day.

The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

The first market can feel like a gut punch. You've paid for a tent, tablecloths, signage, packaging, and a new banner, and suddenly the sales from that first weekend look tiny next to the receipts. It's easy to worry that you made a mistake, even though the setup purchases were the only way to get your booth ready. The pressure is real when the cash box doesn't seem to cover the big upfront spend.

The Education

One-time setup costs should be amortized across the events that benefit from them. Instead of booking the full $600 for a tent, signage, and displays against your first market, spread that $600 over the next 12 markets you plan to attend. That turns it into a $50 per-event cost that makes each event's profitability easier to compare. As you add more events, you can extend the schedule or lower the per-event amount so the investment reflects its true, multi-market value.

The Solution

Keep a simple setup-cost tracker that lists the item, total cost, and how many events it will serve. Each event, record the amortized amount alongside your booth fee, travel, and inventory. That way you see a fair profit picture without panicking after the first show, and you know exactly when a setup item has paid for itself.
