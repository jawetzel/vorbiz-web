Profit insights

Do you track which products are most profitable or just total sales?

Total sales tell you volume, but profit tracking tells you what to keep making.

The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

Some items sell fast but barely cover costs. Others sell fewer units but leave you with strong margins. If you're only tracking total sales, it's hard to know which products are actually driving profit.

The Education

Profit per product requires knowing both price and cost. Even a rough material and labor estimate helps you see which items are worth the time. You don't have to track every item in detail—focus on your top sellers first.

The Solution

Start with a small subset of products and track margin before scaling.

  • Track top five sellers and calculate a simple margin: price minus cost.
  • Use product categories if individual tracking is too detailed.
  • Review profit data before producing new batches so you focus on the best return.

Knowing what truly makes money helps you plan production and pricing with confidence.
