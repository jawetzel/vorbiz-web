Record retention

How long do you keep financial records?

Keeping records long enough protects you and simplifies tax questions.

The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

Receipts, invoices, and statements pile up quickly. You want to stay compliant without keeping endless paper.

The Education

Retention guidelines vary by country, but many small businesses keep tax records for multiple years. Digital storage makes it easier to hold onto records without clutter. Consistent organization matters more than the format.

The Solution

Adopt a simple retention rule and store records digitally.

  • Keep tax returns and supporting records for several years in a secure folder.
  • Store receipts and invoices digitally with clear year-based folders.
  • Archive older records annually to keep your system tidy.

A clear retention habit protects you without creating paper chaos.
