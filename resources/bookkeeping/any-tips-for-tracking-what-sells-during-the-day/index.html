Sales tracking

Any tips for tracking what sells during the day?

Small tracking habits reveal which products truly move when the booth is busy.

The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

Market days are hectic. You're answering questions, making change, and packing orders, and by the end you can't remember which items actually flew off the table. You know that the details would help with restocking, but you don't want a system that slows down checkout.

The Education

The goal is to capture enough detail to see patterns without recording every tiny step. Even a lightweight method—like tally marks per product or a quick note for best sellers—gives you more insight than memory. If you connect your sales to time blocks (morning rush, midday lull), you learn when certain items sell best.

The Solution

Use one of these fast methods that works with a busy booth and still gives you reliable insights.

  • Print a simple product list and use tally marks for each sale; total it during slow moments.
  • Use a POS item grid with a “quick add” button so each tap records the product automatically.
  • Keep a “top three” note on your phone at lunch and at close to capture best sellers by time block.

Pick one method and use it for three events in a row. Consistency is what turns tracking into useful trends.
