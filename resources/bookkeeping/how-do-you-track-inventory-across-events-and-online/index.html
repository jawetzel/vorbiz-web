Inventory tracking

How do you track your inventory across events and online?

A shared inventory view prevents double-selling and helps you restock with confidence.

The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

You sell at markets, take online orders, and occasionally do wholesale, and it's easy for inventory to get out of sync. You don't want to promise an item online that just sold at the booth, or show up at an event with too little stock because online orders pulled from the same bin.

The Education

Inventory accuracy comes from a single source of truth. Whether it's a spreadsheet or a tool, the key is to update it after each sales channel changes. The more sales channels you have, the more valuable real-time updates become.

The Solution

Pick a central inventory list and build a simple update routine around it.

  • Use a master inventory sheet with columns for starting stock, sales by channel, and ending stock.
  • Update inventory immediately after each event and after each online order batch.
  • If you have frequent online sales, consider a tool that syncs POS and online storefront inventory automatically.

Even a basic inventory log keeps you from over-selling and helps you restock the items that actually move.
