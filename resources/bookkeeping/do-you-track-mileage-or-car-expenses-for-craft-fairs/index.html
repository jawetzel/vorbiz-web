Travel tracking

Do you track mileage or car expenses for craft fairs?

Mileage logs protect your deductions and show the true cost of getting to market.

The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

It's easy to forget the miles you drove to a show or the parking you paid. Those costs add up quickly, and if you don't track them, you miss out on real deductions that reduce your taxable income.

The Education

Mileage tracking is usually done with a log that notes date, destination, purpose, and miles driven. Some vendors track actual vehicle expenses instead, but mileage logs are often simpler. Whichever method you use, consistency matters more than perfection.

The Solution

Keep a simple mileage log or app habit tied to every event.

  • Record the starting and ending odometer (or use a mileage app) for each event trip.
  • Store parking and toll receipts with your event paperwork.
  • Review your mileage totals monthly so you don't lose track of business travel.

Tracking travel costs keeps your profit numbers accurate and ensures you claim what you're owed.
