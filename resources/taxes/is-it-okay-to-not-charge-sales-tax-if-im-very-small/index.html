Is it okay to not charge sales tax to customers if I’m very small?

Sales tax rules usually apply regardless of size, so skipping it can create compliance risk.

The Narrative

The Empathy

You only sell occasionally and it feels excessive to register and charge tax. You worry customers will balk at paying a little extra.

The Education

In many states, sales tax applies to taxable goods regardless of your business size. Some areas offer limited exemptions or temporary permits, but skipping tax entirely can lead to penalties if audited. Adding tax at checkout is often accepted when you explain it clearly.

The Solution

Check your local rules and register if required. Add a small sign that says "Sales tax added at checkout" and keep the tax amount tracked separately. This keeps you compliant and removes uncertainty.
