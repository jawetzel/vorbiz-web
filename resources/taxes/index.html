The Empathy

A vendor signs up for their first market and suddenly sees words like "sales tax permit" and "business license" on the event checklist. They're already juggling inventory prep, and now they worry they missed a key form or collected the wrong amount of tax. The rules feel different in every city, and it's hard to tell what is required versus what is optional.

The Education

Explain the core concept: sales tax is collected on behalf of the state, and it must be tracked separately from revenue. Outline the difference between gross sales, sales tax collected, and taxable profit. Mention that permits, filing schedules, and thresholds vary by jurisdiction, but the habit that matters most is keeping clean records of every taxable sale so filings are accurate and stress-free.

The Solution

Encourage a system that captures sales tax automatically, stores receipts, and labels each event with the correct tax rate. When vendors can export a clean summary, filing monthly or quarterly returns becomes a routine instead of a scramble. The system turns confusing tax questions into a repeatable checklist so vendors stay compliant and can focus on selling.
