Slow months

If you have slow months with no events, how do you cover ongoing expenses?

Planning ahead keeps bills paid during quiet periods.

The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

Slow months still come with costs like supplies, subscriptions, and insurance. You need a plan to keep those covered.

The Education

A reserve built during peak months can cover ongoing expenses. Cutting discretionary costs and building alternate sales channels also helps.

The Solution

Use savings and lean budgeting to bridge slow months.

  • Set aside profits from busy months into an expense buffer.
  • Reduce variable spending during slow periods.
  • Offer online or custom orders to supplement income.

A planned buffer keeps slow months from disrupting your business.
