Pricing boundaries

Do you ever haggle or negotiate prices at your booth?

Pricing confidence grows when you set clear boundaries for discounts, so every “maybe” doesn't turn into a profit leak.

The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

A friendly shopper picks up a piece, smiles, and asks, “What's your best price?” You want to be welcoming, but you also hear the voice in your head saying you already priced it fairly. When the next customer is waiting and the booth is busy, it's easy to say yes to a quick discount just to keep the moment warm. Later, you remember that you lowered three prices in a row and wonder if the day was still worth it. That tug-of-war between hospitality and profitability is real.

The Education

Best practice is to decide your pricing boundaries before the market opens. Start with a non-negotiable floor price that covers materials, time, and fees, then define the small, intentional flex you're comfortable with — like a bundle discount, end-of-day markdowns, or a “seconds” bin. That way, the moment doesn't dictate the math. A clear boundary also protects your brand: consistent prices tell shoppers your work is valued and that you don't play favorites.

The Solution

Put a simple negotiation policy in your booth kit. For example: no haggling on core items, a 10% discount on two-or-more bundles, and a hard stop at your floor price. Post a small sign like “Bundle and save” so customers see the option without asking. Keep a quick reference card that lists your floor price by product so you can say “I'm happy to do a set discount” instead of making up numbers on the spot. With boundaries in place, you can stay kind, protect profit, and leave the market knowing you honored both the customer and your business.
