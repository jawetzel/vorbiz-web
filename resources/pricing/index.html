The Narrative (Left Column)

The Empathy

Capture the moment a vendor stares at a price tag and second-guesses everything. They know their work is worth more than the materials, but they worry about scaring away shoppers or being undercut by cheaper booths. Some customers ask, "Is that the best you can do?" and it feels personal. Pricing starts to feel like guesswork instead of a confident decision.

The Education

Break down a simple pricing formula: material cost + labor cost + overhead + profit margin. Show how to value time by assigning an hourly rate and multiplying it by the minutes each item takes to make. Explain that consistent pricing helps a vendor compare sales, see which items pull their weight, and avoid hidden losses from fees, discounts, or card processing. The key takeaway is that pricing is math first, emotions second.

The Solution

Point toward a system that stores each item's cost, labor time, and target margin so pricing decisions are repeatable. With a reliable system, vendors can test price ranges, build bundles, and adjust for event fees without starting from scratch every time. The system makes it easier to stand behind prices, answer customer questions, and protect profit even when the market is slow.
