Pricing guidance

If the market charges a sales commission, should I raise prices?

Commission fees reduce net margin, so decide whether to offset them with pricing or product mix.

The Narrative

The Empathy

A market takes a percentage of every sale, and it feels like a hidden cut into your profits. You are not sure if you should raise prices or just accept the fee as part of doing business.

The Education

Commission fees act like a variable overhead cost. If the market takes 10%, a $50 sale becomes $45. That can be fine if your margins are healthy, but if your margins are thin it can be painful. You have three options: raise prices to preserve your margin, adjust your mix to include higher-margin items, or accept the lower margin in exchange for the venue's traffic. The best approach depends on whether the market provides enough volume or exposure to justify the fee.

The Solution

Run a quick commission impact check: multiply each item price by the commission rate to find your net. If the result drops below your target margin, raise prices slightly across the board or spotlight higher-margin items for that venue. Keep prices consistent across channels when possible, but if the commission is unusually high, consider a venue-specific assortment so the adjustment is less visible. Your goal is a sustainable net margin after fees, not just a top-line price.
