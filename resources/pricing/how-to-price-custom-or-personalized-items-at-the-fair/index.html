Pricing playbook

How do I price custom or personalized items at the fair?

Custom work should feel profitable, not stressful, so build a price that pays for extra time, risk, and client expectations before you say yes.

The Narrative

The Empathy

Someone walks up to your booth and asks for a custom name, a special color, or a one-of-a-kind tweak. You want to say yes, but you can already feel the extra design time, back-and-forth messages, and the risk of being left with an item you cannot resell. It's hard to know if the price you quote will cover the work, especially when the customer is standing right there waiting for an answer.

The Education

Start with your standard item price, then add a customization premium for the extra labor and attention. That premium can be a flat add-on (like $10-$20 for personalization) or an hourly rate applied to your time estimate. Make sure your time estimate includes design time, production time, and a buffer for revisions or mistakes. Deposits protect you when a customer changes their mind, so collect a partial payment up front to cover materials and confirm the order is real. The clearer your time estimate and deposit terms are, the less likely you are to undercharge or get stuck with unpaid work.

The Solution

Build a repeatable custom-order formula: base price + personalization premium + rush fee (if needed). Use a quick intake checklist to confirm the details, quote the time estimate in writing, and note the deposit amount before you start production. When your pricing includes a customization premium, a defined deposit, and a realistic time estimate, you can say yes confidently, protect your schedule, and keep custom work profitable.
