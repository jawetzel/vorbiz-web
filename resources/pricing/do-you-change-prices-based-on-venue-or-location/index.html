Pricing guidance

Do you change your prices based on the venue or location?

Location can shift customer expectations, but consistent pricing protects trust and simplifies your process.

The Narrative

The Empathy

A high-end market downtown feels very different from a small-town pop-up, and it's tempting to change your prices to match the crowd. You don't want to leave money on the table in one place or price yourself out in another. At the same time, changing prices constantly can confuse customers and create awkward comparisons.

The Education

Think about local market fit instead of a full price swap. If a venue attracts higher-spend shoppers, you can highlight premium items, curated sets, or upgraded packaging rather than raising every price. In more price-sensitive venues, lead with entry items and mid-tier bestsellers while keeping your core pricing intact. Consistency matters because customers compare events, and it keeps your inventory labels and checkout flow clean. The goal is to adapt your assortment and presentation, not undermine your baseline pricing.

The Solution

Keep a consistent base price list across venues, then build a market-specific mix: premium focus for upscale venues and value-friendly bundles for community markets. Use signage to guide shoppers to the right tier, and track which mix performs best in each location. When you adjust the offering rather than the price tag, you protect trust, save time on relabeling, and still meet local expectations.
