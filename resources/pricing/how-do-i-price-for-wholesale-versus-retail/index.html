How do I price for wholesale versus retail?

Wholesale pricing needs to cover your costs while leaving enough margin for the retailer.

The Narrative

The Empathy

A shop owner loves your products and wants to stock them, but the wholesale price they need feels too low. You are worried that selling at a discount will wipe out your profit.

The Education

Wholesale typically follows keystone pricing: the wholesale price is about half of the retail price so the retailer can double it and still make margin. That only works if your costs and labor are low enough to allow a 50% wholesale margin. If your cost to make an item is $8 and you retail it for $24, a wholesale price of $12 still gives you a $4 margin. If that is too tight, you may need to raise retail prices, adjust production, or limit which products are available wholesale. Wholesale is a volume play, so make sure the order size and terms justify the lower per-unit margin.

The Solution

Build a wholesale pricing sheet with three numbers: cost, wholesale price, and retail price. Use keystone as a baseline, then validate that your wholesale margin still meets your target. Offer a tight, wholesale-friendly assortment rather than every SKU. Clear pricing tiers and minimum order quantities protect your time while making retail partners happy.
